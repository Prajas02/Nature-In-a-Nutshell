Rainbow Formation-In A Nutshell

Here we will try to explain the beautiful formation of rainbow in as simple language as possible.So Let's get into it.

What exactly is a Rainbow?

Rainbow is a meteorological phenomenon that is caused by reflection, refraction and dispersion of white light in water droplets resulting in a spectrum of light appearing in the sky. It takes the form of a multicoloured circular arc. Rainbows caused by sunlight always appear in the section of sky directly opposite the Sun.

Rainbows can be full circles. However, the observer normally sees only an arc formed by illuminated droplets above the ground.(As you can see in the image below).

What are the componets of a Rainbow?

There are 4 types of SOlar Eclipse in total:-A rainbow is formed from a white beam of of light(as noted above).This white light is actually a mixture of a seven colours of light. These colours are given below:- Violet Indigo Blue Green Yellow Orange Red These seven colours above comprises the spectrum of light. Together in short they are reffered to as "VIBGYOR"with each letter represting the first letter of the color order wise.

How a Rainbow is formed.

Conditions required for the formation of rainbow are:

  • Presence of raindrops.
  • Sun should be at your back to observe the rainbow.

Process of rainbow formation

  • The raindrops present in the sky acts as many smaller prisms in the sky.
  • As the white light enters and leaves the raindrops, the different colored rays are refracted by different amounts due to which an arc of seven colors (VIBGYOR) is formed in the sky.(dispersion of white light)

Explanation

  1. A ray of white light(yellow-colored in the figure) enters the rain droplet.
  2. On touching the surface of the droplet, it undergoes refraction and is dispersed into its seven components to form a spectrum of light. (for simplicity only two colors 'red' and 'violet' are shown).
  3. This spectrum of light undergoes total internal reflection on striking the internal surface of the droplet and finally refracts again at the surface of the droplet and moves out.
  4. Due to this refraction, the rainbow is formed at the opposite side is the sun.

Some myths and beliefs about rainbow

In myths and legends around the world the rainbow has played a magical, otherworldly symbol, whether as a bridge linking man to heaven, a messenger from the gods, a promise from God, an archer’s bow used by the gods, or a god-like serpent that could be ridden to the heavens. Below are some of the myths and legends associated with the rainbow:

  • It is believed by the people that at the place where the arc of rainbow touches the ground a pot full of golds and many invaluable treasures are hidden.
  • The rainbow is depicted as an archer's bow in Hindu mythology. Indra, the god of thunder and war, uses the rainbow to shoot arrows of lightning trying to protect humanity from the evil forces(or rakshas).
  • The Rainbow Serpent myth is believed by some to represent the world’s oldest continuous religious tradition in australia.According to the myth, the Rainbow Serpent descended from that larger universe being visible as a dark streak in the Milky Way. It revealed itself to people in this world as a rainbow as it moved through water and the rain, shaping landscapes, naming and singing of places, dreaming them into existence by showing a rainbow(sign of joy and happiness).
  • In Mayan mythology the serpent was also a very important social and religious symbol. Mayan mythology describes serpents as being the vehicles by which celestial bodies, such as the sun and stars, cross the heavens. They could do so by walking on the rainbow which lead them straight to the doors of heavens.
